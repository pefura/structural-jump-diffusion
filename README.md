DtD-TB: Individualized Mortality Risk Assessment Tool
DtD-TB is an interactive clinical triage tool designed to predict mortality risk in patients with tuberculosis at the time of admission.
Unlike traditional statistical models that only provide general risk factors, this tool uses a structural framework inspired by reliability engineering. It conceptualizes a patient’s health as a dynamic system and calculates a "safety buffer" representing how far a patient is from a critical physiological failure threshold.
Key Features
Individualized Assessment: Calculates a personalized risk profile based on specific patient characteristics.
Mechanistic Approach: Models health as a continuous process subject to steady recovery trends, metabolic instability, and sudden clinical shocks.
Distance-to-Death (DtD) Score: Provides a standardized metric indicating the patient's physiological solvency.
Real-time Triage: Instantly categorizes patients into four clinical priority levels.
Clinical Triage Levels
The application stratifies patients into four risk tiers based on their calculated 240-day probability of mortality:
🔴 Level 1: Critical Risk (Risk > 10%) - Immediate intensive clinical and nutritional intervention required.
🟠 Level 2: High Risk (Risk 5-10%) - Close physiological monitoring and frequent follow-up required.
🔵 Level 3: Moderate Risk (Risk 2.5-5%) - Regular monitoring of health capital and weight progression.
🟢 Level 4: Stable / Low Risk (Risk < 2.5%) - High biological solvency; follow standard tuberculosis care protocols.
